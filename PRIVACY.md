# Privacy Policy for Social Media Blocker

Effective date: July 6, 2026

Social Media Blocker is a Chrome extension that blocks selected social media websites until you write a reason for access.

## Data the extension handles

The extension may store the following information locally in Chrome extension storage:

- Whether blocking is enabled globally.
- Which supported social media sites are enabled or disabled.
- The supported social media site or URL you attempted to visit.
- The reason you entered before continuing to a blocked site.
- The date and time the reason was recorded.

The extension keeps up to 200 recent access-reason records. Older records are removed automatically as new records are added.

## How the data is used

This data is used only to provide the extension's blocking, per-site settings, access-grant, and recent-reasons history features.

## Data sharing and sales

Social Media Blocker does not sell user data. It does not send your stored settings, blocked URLs, timestamps, or reasons to the developer, advertisers, analytics providers, or any external server.

## Third-party requests

The block page loads decorative outdoor photos from Unsplash. Those image requests are made directly by your browser to Unsplash and may provide Unsplash with standard request information such as your IP address, browser user agent, and the requested image URL. The extension does not send your stored access reasons or settings to Unsplash.

## Data storage and deletion

Data is stored locally by Chrome for this extension. You can delete it by clearing the extension's storage/data in Chrome or by uninstalling the extension.
